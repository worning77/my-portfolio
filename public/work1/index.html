Failed experience, at least I tried

My First Attempt

The first idea was born in the summer of 2015 and officially announced death in July, 2016. I tried my best to see it through although I might seem to be a newbie in this thriving industry. Nevertheless, it failed due to many reasons. This is my one-year-long story.

Background

In 2015, WeChat, a messaging app as well as life moments sharing app, dominated and still is dominating the application market in China. Users share their breakfast, the vacation places they hit, their own selfie and stuff on it. I found it boring and yearning for something fun and educational. I asked myself, why couldnt I create a platform sharing something meaningful, like skills? And more importantly, why not share them in the form of short videos? I got a crazy idea.

Chapter 0

    Except Users in Zaihang will meet and talk in real life.
    4. The target users of every platform are highly related to the main topics they made at the beginning stage.

    In the short video field, we found that:

    None of them uses videos to spread knowledge.

    Some issues in videoing progress

    From a professor

    A famous Chinese literature professor answered our 5 prepared questions, he couldn't make it clear in 5 min, since he sometimes went off the topic.

    When we let other students watch this 30min-long video. At first they had interests, but they felt tired gradually.

    We underestimated the difficulty of making a knowledge-spread video in most cases!

    We never foresaw such situations before. And these difficulties calmed us down. After discussions with several professional product managers in Tencent, we reconsidered our program and thought about following questions.

    Why Q&A platforms like zhihu.com didn't have a video function?

    Before: We had thought Zhihu was a place where people can share their views, and they can be logical if written down.

    Reality: Zhihu didn't expect users to be able to make a good video at all. If by videoing, it would lose many potential users.

    Actually, Zhihu started to hold video sharing activiting offically after one year, because they already had enough loyal users

    Video or Meaningful social network?

    Before: We had thought video+knowledge+existing social circle would be perfect, because video was the most effective spreading way.

    Reality: Chinese didn't have the habit of making videos at that time yet. Most companies were trying to develop it by using live stream.

    In fact, after more than one-year trial of live steam, all of those still existing products focus on short video, since many hosts have already had the ability to produce well-made videos, and useres are willing to watch them.

    It seemed that we emphasized too much about the video function and forgot the original wish of creating a valuable social network.

    We had no choice but removed the video function.

    H5 APP

    The first sharing activity

    We chose the most common topic among college students--How to make a good PPT as the first topic of our WeChat online sharing activity. We used WeChat official account for publicity based on its all-round friends circle, and finally attracted 123 students. After that, we publicized our APP and invited those students who had interests as the seed users to test the App.

  • First, we introduced today's host, in order to attract someone who knew him.

  • Second, we also put some examples to illustrate his ppt-design ability. It might attract more potential users.( It was a way to imitate those students who had questions only when they saw something and then got inspired. )

    • The whole activity lasted for 1.5h. During that, only not more than 10 people asked related questions after the host finished his skill-sharing, which was far from our expectation.

    Results of the activity and user test

    Reflections:
    1. It was an H5 App, and the interface was not appealing;
    2. People dont have that many questions in their mind;
    3. If there are not enough friends or acquaintances using this platform, it doesnt have any value.
    4. We need to discover the real seed users of our platform.

    Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

    Putting aside the form of application, we decided to experiment on our idea. We held a sharing activity among our WeChat friends and followers and made an observation.

    In the following weeks, we held 5 WeChat sharing activities online. The results of these experiments were perfect, as well as the feedbacks from students who took part in -- 4 students we were not familiar with wanted to be a host to share their skills if possible. The average number of audience was 10, the average lasting time was 1 hour.

    That proved our original idea was right and needed--"Spread knowledge by videoing","Ask friends or friends' friends for ideas and suggestions of how to get started a new interest", "A place where we could share our skills without any sharing stress"

    June

    The End

    The sharing activities couldn't last if we didn't have much time spent in coming up with topics, inviting hosts and organizing events. With the approach of the final exam, our team could no longer hang in there and I dissembled the team.

    Review

    Our idea has been proved right by the MVP, but we found some reasons why we failed to make such a platform we envisioned:

  • We were not a professional team. As for leaders, we put too many functions in a new App in the beignning stage, and thus we changed the idea too many times which made the programmers feel exhausted. And till the end, our inexperienced programmer didn't make a good APP, but he tried. ( We were not in the beginning of Facebook time )
  • We knew that it was hard for us to design and code an app, but before we verify our idea, we would not use money to buy an APP coded by professionals, since I saw too many heavily-invested APP died because of the lack of users.
  • We underestimated the difficulty of copying the existing friends connection on a brand new platform, since WeChat (Tencent) would never let his social network chain go, and even till now, it control Chinese social net firmly.
  • The delemma: the vertical fields made friends in real life lose its value; the all-round platform heavily relied on friends connection.
  • We have not yet found a brilliant way as cold boot( like what SnapChat did at that time )to attract users.

    • As for my work, I don't think I was a qualified leader or product manager. I made too many mistakes in the whole process. For example, before I did a user research, I started to think about how to make a promotional film to spread my idea and attract users. Since I blindly believed in Agile Development, I didn't do well in the preparation ( site map and all sketch pages ) for programmers. As a UI designer, I focused on too many details at the beginning. Meanwhile, I failed to manage our time. But, in this whole process, I've learned a lot, which helped me realize my weaknesses and pushed me to perfect myself both as a leader and a product designer.
